About CATSystem

The word CATSystem is acronym for Cancer Tracking System. It is a research project by The University of Kansas Medical Center (KUMC) in collaboration with Global Health Innovation Foundation (GHI) and Kenya Medical Research Institute (KEMRI).

The CATSystem project aims to develop a robust clinical management tool/system that will strengthen Cervical Cancer Screening, Treatment, and Referral procedures in Kenya.

Goals of CATSystem Project

The CATSystem project aims to:

  •  Bridge the gap where most patients fall-out due to inadequate follow-up through the use of a web-based Cancer Tracking System.
  •  Digitalize the documentation process through the use of an electronic Cervical Cancer Screening Form.
  •  Ensure regular cervical cancer screening is done for all women of reproductive health.
  •  Deploy the use of technology to facilitate efficient communication between the providers and the patient through the use of SMS text messaging.
  •  Deploy the use of SMS text messaging to send out reminders about follow-up visits and other health related information to patients.
  •  Provide a platform for accurate reporting by generating monthly Reporting tools for example, Kenya MOH 711 – Cervical Cancer Screening section.
  •  Include the ability to generate and print a Cervical Cancer Screening and Treatment Daily Activity Register.
